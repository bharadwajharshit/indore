INDORE - The Foodie Junction

Indore is a largest city in central India known for its food, cleanliness, industries and climate. Indore has won title of India's cleanest city continuously for 4 times. It is also regarded as the commercial capital of Madhya Pradesh state. Indore is a well known city that is considered as educational hub in India and the first city having campuses of both IIM and IIT. Indore also stands proudly to have Indian Army cantonment known as MHOW (Military Headquarter of War) and also serves as a home to three eminent institutes viz. Army War College, Infantry School and Military College of Telecommunication Engineering. All three institutes are established in MHOW and many Indian Army personnels are trained here. The Border Security Force (BSF) has its Central School of Weapons and Tactics (CSWT) at Indore.

The city of Indore was established by Malhar Rao Holkar, Commander-in-Chief of the Maratha army in the year 1732, On 29 July 1732, Peshwa Bajirao granted Malwa province to Malhar Rao Holkar by merging 28 and a half parganas to Malhar Rao Holkar, the founding ruler of the Holkar dynasty and then established city of Indore. His daughter-in-law Ahilyabai Holkar moved the state's capital to Maheshwar in 1767, but Indore remained an important commercial and military centre. The city was ruled by other Holkar rulers viz. Maharaja Shivaji Rao Holkar, Maharaja Khande Rao Holkar, Maharaja Tukoji Rao Holkar, Maharaja Yeshwant Rao Holkar who continued to rule and developed the city that majorly includes town development, trade and transportation, city expansion etc. After the independence of India in 1947, Indore State, along with a number of neighbouring princely states, acceded to India. Yashwant Rao Holkar II, the last ruler of the state, signed the instrument of accession to the Indian Union on 1 January 1950. The territories of the state became part of the new Indian state of Madhya Bharat.

Climate & Demographics

Indore is the most populous city in the Madhya Pradesh. Indore is also the largest metropolitan city in Central India. It has population density of around 25,700 persons per sqr. km. In 2018, the estimated population of the Indore was about 4.2 million. Hindi is the majorly spoken language in the city along with Marathi, Sindhi, Malwi and Nimadi as other languages and dialects.

Indore lies on a borderline between a humid subtropical climate and a tropical savanna climate. Because of its high elevation and inland location even during the hottest months the nights are relatively cool, which is known as Shab-e-Malwa.

Exploring Indore City

Indore has been a historic city established since 1732 in the reign of Maratha empire. Apart from the historic royal monuments of Holkar rule, there are many sights of ancient religious interests, places of natural importance and places depicting modern culture including malls, pubs etc. Along side of the places, Indore happens to be the food cluster in central India region and because of variety in food taste, the city is also considered to be the food capital of India.

Whenever one visits Indore, the person falls in love with the city. There are many reasons behind this which we will be exploring below.

Historic Sights

Indore and its surroundings are the home of various visiting spots starting from the ancient era to the modern history, that includes royal palaces inside the Indore city and some forts in Mandu and Maheshwar being located outside the city.

Rajwada is seven storey building and was the royal court of Holkar rule and was constructed in year 1766. Later, the Holkar Queen Devi Ahilyabai Holkar moved the capital of Holkar regime to Maheshwar and ruled in the region from Maheshwar fort for 28 long years.

The construction of Royal Lalbagh Palace was started in year 1886 by Maharaja Shivaji Rao Holkar. It is surrounded in an area of 28 acres land full of greenery and has a famous red rose garden. The palace is decorated by marble pillars and paintings of Indian and Italian styles on walls and ceilings.

Other than these, there are monuments like Phooti Kothi, Hawa Bunglow, Sukhniwas Palace that depict the history of Holkar-British rule.

Religious Interests

The city is named after its Indreshwar Mahadev Temple, where Indra is the presiding deity. It is believed that Indra himself did Tapasya (meditation) in this place and led sage Swami Indrapuri to establish the temple. Later, Maharaja Tukoji Rao Holkar renovated the temple.

Indore is a secular city that has sights of all religious interests. Famous sites are shown below.

Khajrana Ganesh Mandir is one of the oldest temples of the city. This temple was constructed during the reign of Holkar queen Devi Ahilya bai. Ranjeet Hanuman Mandir, Bijasan Mata Mandir, Annapoorna Mata Mandir, Vaishnav Devi Dham, Gopal Mandir, ISKCON Temple are some other notable Hindu sites.

Gurudwara Imli Sahib is one of the oldest gurudwara in Indore. It is believed that once Shri Gurunanak Ji visited Indore in year 1568 and performed one of the total of 4 Udaasis here. There was a Tamarind (Imli) tree near the bank of river Kanh under which Shri GuruNanak ji sat and meditated, later on a gurudwara was constructed at the same place and hence got it's name as Gurudwara Imli Sahib. There are also many more famous gurudwaras in the city viz. Gurudwara Topkhana Sahib, Gurudwara GuruNanak Satsang Darbar etc.

Nahar Shah Wali Dargah is one of the most ancient Islamic shrine in Indore. It was established by Hazrat Syed Gajibuddin Iraqi Rehmatullah Ali around 400 years ago. It is believed that Hazrat Iraqi came to Indore from Iraq. Other Islamic shrines include Madina Masjid, Karbala Maidan Masjid, Masjid-E-Saifee etc.

Kanch(Glass) Mandir is one of the most attractive temples in Indore. It is special because of its construction from glass. It was constructed in 1903 by Seth Hukumchand Jain. Seth Hukumchand was one of the most prominent indurtrialists of Indore. Other Jain shrines include Hinkargiri and Gommatgiri.

Nature

It is always pleasent being surrounded by nature. Every person has an urge to go for outing, camping and explore places in the lap of mother nature. Indore, being a city of Malwa Plateau, has many natural sights seeing spots around the city. People find time to explore and go out with family, friends and even alone sometimes. Many adventurous institute organise trekking and camping activities and trips to these places. There a long list of such spots that includes waterfalls, lakes and wildlife. This is a collection of names like Patalpani Waterfall, Tinchha waterfalls, Mohadi falls, Jogi Bhadak, Bamniya Kund, Ralamandal Wildlife Sanctuary, Sirpur Lake and many more.

Located in Mhow Tehsil, Patalpani Waterfall is approximately 300 feet high. A hidden gem, it is a favourite picnic spot among families with its majestic beauty. During rainy seasons, the water gushes through great force, and hence, it is also ideal for specific adventure activities. A peaceful atmosphere offers a beautiful option to unwind for families. While you are visiting Patalpani Waterfall, make sure you also visit the Maheshwar, Omkareshwar and other pilgrim sites in the region. Plan a picnic around the July to October months to capture the true essence of the place.

Gulawat is a beautiful lotus valley located about 30 kms away from city of Indore. Gulawat village is located in Hatod tehsil of Indore district. Gulawat Lotus Lake offers breathtaking views of the lotus valley and is therefore a place of great interest for photographers too. The valley is a perfect weekend getaway place for Indoreans as the lake is surrounded by greenery which makes the environment serene. Located at one end of Yashwant Sagar in Indore the valley is very beautiful and attracts various migrating birds. The lake with lots of surfaced lotuses is one of the most beautiful views one can come across. Sunrise, boating, horse riding and the temple are some of the other attractions. A 100-metre-long bridge over the lake gives travellers a perfect view of the lake and beautiful lotuses. A little ahead, a bamboo forest adds to the already mesmerizing experience.

It is a famous tourist spot located on the Indore-Mumbai highway. The Janapav Kuti is a mountain that offers a spectacular view of the area where you can go for a walk around or even trek your way to find a quiet spot to lay out your picnic. Around Diwali, a mega fair is also held annually which is a must-visit gala. The highest point of the Malwa plateau is a beautiful getaway during the rainy season. It is the birthplace of Lord Parshurama, the sixth avatar of Lord Vishnu, and is considered sacred by the Hindu community. At the top of the hill, there is an ashram of Jamadagni.

The region around the Choral Dam is blissfully peaceful at any time of the year. There is ample space where you can spread out your picnic blanket to spend a lazy day with a magnificent sunrise or sunset as a backdrop to your family photograph of the day. The cool breeze in this hilly area is soothing while you munch on your favourite snacks with your near and dear ones.

For bird photography enthusiasts, a visit to the Sirpur Lake is mandatory. Declared as an IBA (an Important Bird Area), the lake receives around 150 different species of migratory as well as resident birds. To name a few, Eurasian Wigeon, Brahminy Shelduck, Zitting Cisticola, Open Billed Stork, Purple Heron, Short-Eared Owl, Singing Bushlark, Greater Flamingo and Bluethroat are some of the birds that can be spotted here. A butterfly park is also in the pipeline as a part of an ambitious plan for the Sirpur Lake. The Lake is spread over a whopping 800 acres. Escape the daily hustle and bustle of the mundane life to this peaceful green retreat with family and spend a day in nature's company.

This beautiful wildlife and bird sanctuary is an ideal picnic spot for nature lovers. Established in the year 1981 for the conservation of flora and fauna in the region, Rala Mandal is 20 kms away from the city of Indore. Inside the sanctuary, there is also a palace which was built by the Holkers during their reign. To have a good view of the elephants, deer and others among animals and eucalyptus, teak, Chandan, Babool among the trees in the forest, one can opt for a camel ride. The perfect time to plan a picnic at Rala Mandal is between October and February.

Transportation

Transportation and connectivity with other places plays a vital role in development of any city. Indore, in means of connectivity and transporation is very well connected to mostly each and every city of India all by means of air, railways, roads. The city public transport is also very efficient and effective.

Modes of Transportation in Indore City
AIRWAYS: Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar Airport (IATA: IDR, ICAO: VAID) is an international airport that principally serves Indore and adjacent regions in the Indian State of Madhya Pradesh. It is the busiest airport in Central India and is located 8 kms west of Indore. According to the statistics released by the Airports Authority of India, the agency responsible for the maintenance and management of the airport, it is the 18th busiest airport in India by passenger traffic year 2018-19. Indore airport has won this award for 'Best Regional Airport in Asia Pacific' in the list of 98 airports of 18 countries.
RAILWAYS: Indore Junction (station code: INDB) is one of the Railway junctions in Madhya Pradesh and serves Indore, the commercial capital of Central India. The railway station of Indore Junction BG falls under the administrative control of Western Railway Zone of Indian Railways. It consists of 6 main railway platforms. The Station is located 1 km away from the city centre. It is one of the ISO Certified Railway Stations of India. Suburbans Railway Stations include Choral, Patalpani, MHOW, Rau, Rajendra Nagar Terminus, Saify Nagar, Lokmanya Nagar, LaxmiBai Nagar, Mangliya, Kshipra.
Indore is connected to other parts of India through National and State highways. The National Highways passing through the city are:
  • National Highway 52 (NH 52) contains parts of the erstwhile NH3 (AB Rd stands for Agra Bombay Road) and it originates at Sangrur, Punjab and runs through Jaipur, Rajasthan, Indore, Dhule and terminates at Ankola in Karnataka.
  • National Highway 47 starts from Bamanbore, Gujarat and reaches Indore via Ahmedabad and further connects Betul to terminate at Nagpur.

The state highways passing through the city are:

  • MP State Highway 27 runs from Jhalawar in Rajasthan, through Ujjain, Indore, Burhanpur in Madhya Pradesh and terminates at Malkapur, Buldhana in Maharashtra.
  • MP State Highway 31 (Neemuch – Ratlam – Dhar)
Indore's City Bus transport system runs through 277 km (172 mi) of road with a daily ridership of over 1.4 lakh. Atal Indore City Transport Services Ltd, a PPP scheme operates buses and radio taxis in the city. The buses designated as City Bus operate on 64 Routes with a fleet of 361 buses, with 421 bus stops. The buses are color-coded into three colors: Blue, Magenta and Orange according to their route. Indore BRTS (iBUS) – Indore BRTS is a bus rapid transit system with 53 air-conditioned. Buses are equipped with services like GPS and IVR which are used to track the position of the bus with information displayed on LED displays installed at the bus stops. Indore Magic (Auto Rickshaw) – Indore Auto Rickshaw is a magic service for small distance travel. Daily approx. 5 lakh people travel within the city. Indore Metro is a light metro system that is currently under construction in its first phase.

Commerce & Industries In Indore

The presence of a large number of well known Industries in Indore have made the place a major industrial center of the state. Indore is known as a commercial capital of Madhya Pradesh. Indore is one of the richest cities in central India and also known as "Mini Mumbai" among native people of Indore, due to its lifestyle similarities with Mumbai.

Indore economy is expanding in all directions and it includes both the traditional agro industries and modern corporate and IT companies. One of the busiest cities of Madhya Pradesh, Indore is the economic nerve center of the state. With the flourishing of Indore economy, a number of management and engineering schools have been opened in Indore in order to supply the growing demand of professionals.

Textile or garment industry is the traditional business of the region and it is still an integral part of Indore economy. There are a number of textile mills in the region and Indore still is the bastion of wholesale garments. There are a number of firms involved in export of textile and such business firms contribute a lot to the development of Indore economy. Besides textile, metal industries are also an integral part of Indore economy and it includes, alloy, automobile and steel industries.

Besides such traditional economic activities, Indore is gaining pace as a Tire II city of India. A testing ground for many companies, Indore economy also witnesses a very strong corporate presence. With the boom in the sector of Information Technology, a number of new small and big IT industries have come up in the city which are offering a numerous jobs. A separate electronic complex has been created in Indore which exemplifies the importance of IT in Indore economy. This complex houses offices of some of the top IT companies of India.
