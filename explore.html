Exploring Indore City

Indore has been a historic city established since 1732 in the reign of Maratha empire. Apart from the historic royal monuments of Holkar rule, there are many sights of ancient religious interests, places of natural importance and places depicting modern culture including malls, pubs etc. Along side of the places, Indore happens to be the food cluster in central India region and because of variety in food taste, the city is also considered to be the food capital of India.

Whenever one visits Indore, the person falls in love with the city. There are many reasons behind this which we will be exploring below.

    Historic Sights

    Indore and its surroundings are the home of various visiting spots starting from the ancient era to the modern history, that includes royal palaces inside the Indore city and some forts in Mandu and Maheshwar being located outside the city.

    Rajwada is seven storey building and was the royal court of Holkar rule and was constructed in year 1766. Later, the Holkar Queen Devi Ahilyabai Holkar moved the capital of Holkar regime to Maheshwar and ruled in the region from Maheshwar fort for 28 long years.

    The construction of Royal Lalbagh Palace was started in year 1886 by Maharaja Shivaji Rao Holkar. It is surrounded in an area of 28 acres land full of greenery and has a famous red rose garden. The palace is decorated by marble pillars and paintings of Indian and Italian styles on walls and ceilings.

    Other than these, there are monuments like Phooti Kothi, Hawa Bunglow, Sukhniwas Palace that depict the history of Holkar-British rule.

    Religious Interests

    The city is named after its Indreshwar Mahadev Temple, where Indra is the presiding deity. It is believed that Indra himself did Tapasya (meditation) in this place and led sage Swami Indrapuri to establish the temple. Later, Maharaja Tukoji Rao Holkar renovated the temple.

    Indore is a secular city that has sights of all religious interests. Famous sites are shown below.

    Khajrana Ganesh Mandir is one of the oldest temples of the city. This temple was constructed during the reign of Holkar queen Devi Ahilya bai. Ranjeet Hanuman Mandir, Bijasan Mata Mandir, Annapoorna Mata Mandir, Vaishnav Devi Dham, Gopal Mandir, ISKCON Temple are some other notable Hindu sites.

    Gurudwara Imli Sahib is one of the oldest gurudwara in Indore. It is believed that once Shri Gurunanak Ji visited Indore in year 1568 and performed one of the total of 4 Udaasis here. There was a Tamarind (Imli) tree near the bank of river Kanh under which Shri GuruNanak ji sat and meditated, later on a gurudwara was constructed at the same place and hence got it's name as Gurudwara Imli Sahib. There are also many more famous gurudwaras in the city viz. Gurudwara Topkhana Sahib, Gurudwara GuruNanak Satsang Darbar etc.

    Nahar Shah Wali Dargah is one of the most ancient Islamic shrine in Indore. It was established by Hazrat Syed Gajibuddin Iraqi Rehmatullah Ali around 400 years ago. It is believed that Hazrat Iraqi came to Indore from Iraq. Other Islamic shrines include Madina Masjid, Karbala Maidan Masjid, Masjid-E-Saifee etc.

    Kanch(Glass) Mandir is one of the most attractive temples in Indore. It is special because of its construction from glass. It was constructed in 1903 by Seth Hukumchand Jain. Seth Hukumchand was one of the most prominent indurtrialists of Indore. Other Jain shrines include Hinkargiri and Gommatgiri.

    Nature

    It is always pleasent being surrounded by nature. Every person has an urge to go for outing, camping and explore places in the lap of mother nature. Indore, being a city of Malwa Plateau, has many natural sights seeing spots around the city. People find time to explore and go out with family, friends and even alone sometimes. Many adventurous institutes organise trekking and camping activities and trips to these places. There a long list of such spots that includes waterfalls, lakes and wildlife. This is a collection of names like Patalpani Waterfall, Tinchha waterfalls, Mohadi falls, Jogi Bhadak, Bamniya Kund, Ralamandal Wildlife Sanctuary, Sirpur Lake and many more.

    Located in Mhow Tehsil, Patalpani Waterfall is approximately 300 feet high. A hidden gem, it is a favourite picnic spot among families with its majestic beauty. During rainy seasons, the water gushes through great force, and hence, it is also ideal for specific adventure activities. A peaceful atmosphere offers a beautiful option to unwind for families. While you are visiting Patalpani Waterfall, make sure you also visit the Maheshwar, Omkareshwar and other pilgrim sites in the region. Plan a picnic around the July to October months to capture the true essence of the place.

    Gulawat is a beautiful lotus valley located about 30 kms away from city of Indore. Gulawat village is located in Hatod tehsil of Indore district. Gulawat Lotus Lake offers breathtaking views of the lotus valley and is therefore a place of great interest for photographers too. The valley is a perfect weekend getaway place for Indoreans as the lake is surrounded by greenery which makes the environment serene. Located at one end of Yashwant Sagar in Indore the valley is very beautiful and attracts various migrating birds. The lake with lots of surfaced lotuses is one of the most beautiful views one can come across. Sunrise, boating, horse riding and the temple are some of the other attractions. A 100-metre-long bridge over the lake gives travellers a perfect view of the lake and beautiful lotuses. A little ahead, a bamboo forest adds to the already mesmerizing experience.

    It is a famous tourist spot located on the Indore-Mumbai highway. The Janapav Kuti is a mountain that offers a spectacular view of the area where you can go for a walk around or even trek your way to find a quiet spot to lay out your picnic. Around Diwali, a mega fair is also held annually which is a must-visit gala. The highest point of the Malwa plateau is a beautiful getaway during the rainy season. It is the birthplace of Lord Parshurama, the sixth avatar of Lord Vishnu, and is considered sacred by the Hindu community. At the top of the hill, there is an ashram of Jamadagni.

    The region around the Choral Dam is blissfully peaceful at any time of the year. There is ample space where you can spread out your picnic blanket to spend a lazy day with a magnificent sunrise or sunset as a backdrop to your family photograph of the day. The cool breeze in this hilly area is soothing while you munch on your favourite snacks with your near and dear ones.

    For bird photography enthusiasts, a visit to the Sirpur Lake is mandatory. Declared as an IBA (an Important Bird Area), the lake receives around 150 different species of migratory as well as resident birds. To name a few, Eurasian Wigeon, Brahminy Shelduck, Zitting Cisticola, Open Billed Stork, Purple Heron, Short-Eared Owl, Singing Bushlark, Greater Flamingo and Bluethroat are some of the birds that can be spotted here. A butterfly park is also in the pipeline as a part of an ambitious plan for the Sirpur Lake. The Lake is spread over a whopping 800 acres. Escape the daily hustle and bustle of the mundane life to this peaceful green retreat with family and spend a day in nature's company.

    This beautiful wildlife and bird sanctuary is an ideal picnic spot for nature lovers. Established in the year 1981 for the conservation of flora and fauna in the region, Rala Mandal is 20 kms away from the city of Indore. Inside the sanctuary, there is also a palace which was built by the Holkers during their reign. To have a good view of the elephants, deer and others among animals and eucalyptus, teak, Chandan, Babool among the trees in the forest, one can opt for a camel ride. The perfect time to plan a picnic at Rala Mandal is between October and February.

    Food and Nightlife

    Indore has a happening nightlife and food culture. Food being the speciality of the city makes it the food capital of India. One can very easily find each and every taste of the whole country within the city. Sarafa Bazaar, when in daytime is known for the jewellery business, in night time Sarafa Bazaar is known for the food. 56 Dukaan, is well known for it's significance of serving best tastes in the heart of the city. Starting the breakfast with Poha-Jalebi, Samosa-Kachori to many types of paranthas, followed by lunch with delighting taste of Daal-Bafla alongs with delicious desserts, and ending the day with mesmerizing dinner with a long list of courses. Dahiwada, Bhutte ka Kees, Maalpua, Shikanji are some must have desserts of Indore.

    "In INDORE, there is an end for the day, but not for FOOD..."

    It is an unending discussion when it comes to food of Indore. It always better to experience by oneself instead of discussing over anthing. So, pack up your bags and experience the royal history, divinity, serene nature and delicious food. INDORE IS CALLING, EXPERIENCE INDORE.
